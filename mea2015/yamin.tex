\frame
{
  \ft{General Impressions}

  \begin{block}{Motivated and built up nicely}
    \bi
    \item Empirical evidence is convincing
    \item Theoretically convincing with Phillips curve expressions
    \item Compare simulation results with data 
    \ei
  \end{block}

  \begin{block}{Outcomes}
    \bi
    \item LCPb (Local currency pricing in retail sector whose good is an aggregate of a traded (PCP) non-traded good): 
      \bi \item High inflation persistence and low inflation volatility \ei
    \item Price rigidity home and abroad:
      \be
      \item affects persistence and volatility
      \item asymmetric Calvo stickiness can explain differences in persistence and volatility
      \ee
    \ei
  \end{block}

  \begin{block}{Quality contribution}
    Destined for a quality journal
  \end{block}
}

\frame
{
  \ft{International Explanations}
  \begin{block}{Overwhelming}
  \bi
  \item Four open economy extensions... 
  \item Plus... two types of LCP, closed/open, asymmetric population size, asymmetric price stickiness)
  \item A lot of combinations considered.
  \ei
  \end{block}

  \begin{block}{Open economy extensions}
    \bi
    \item \textit{Only LCP with retail sector (LCPb)} seems explain differences in inflation dynamics
    \item Theoretical foundations suggest they can all play a role
    \item Little differences and little discussion for the many other combinations considered.
    \item What is significant about LCPa vs LCPb, in terms of inflation dynamics.
    \item Is LCPb what sets developed vs developing countries apart?
    \ei
  \end{block}
}

\frame
{
  \ft{Matching is unconvincing}
  \begin{block}{Developed countries}
  \bi
  \item Can't match persistence, but why should they?
  \item With good reason, typical explanations are excluded.
  \ei
  \end{block}
  
  \begin{block}{Developing Countries}
  \bi
  \item Do a better job matching persistence; volatility less so
  \item Differences in policy targets and persistence are excluded.
  \item Asymmetric Calvo stickiness is unsatisfying, misaligned with paper's purpose.
  \item DSGE is calibrated to a developed country.
  \ei
  \end{block}
  
  \begin{block}{``Matching'' is too ambitious}
  \bi
  \item Does not align with the purpose of the paper.
  \item More metrics on explanatory power of international dimensions.
  \ei
  \end{block}
}

\frame

  \ft{Related Questions}
  \begin{footnotesize}
  \textbf{How does aid, political instability, and the interaction affect...}
  \begin{block}{Financial capital inflows?}
    \bi
    \item Aid + political stability $\rightarrow$ increases the productivity of transforming financial capital to physical capital.
    \item Higher return on this productivity may increase the supply of capital inflows.
    \item Reinforcing mechanism.
    \ei
  \end{block}
  \begin{block}{Marginal product of capital?}
    \bi
    \item Specifically, capital that is produced with foreign aid:
    \bdm \hat{I}_{i,t}^{Aid} = \hat{\alpha}_3 \mbox{Aid}_{i,t} + \hat{\alpha}_4 \mbox{Aid}_{i,t}^2 + \hat{\alpha}_5 Aid_{i,t} PI_{i,t} \edm
    \item Growth regression:
    \bdm g_t = \beta_0 + \beta_1(L) I_{i,t} + \beta_2(L) \hat{I}_{i,t}^{Aid} + \beta_3(L) \hat{I}_{i,t}^{Aid} PI_{i,t} + \beta_4' X_{i,t} + \beta_5  S_i + \beta_6 W_t + u_{i,t} \edm
    \ei
  \end{block}
  \end{footnotesize}
}

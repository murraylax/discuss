\frame{\titlepage \setcounter{framenumber}{0}}

\frame
{
  \ft{Summary}
  \uncover<+->{
  \begin{block}{Gender bias issue} 
  Choice of a head of household to seek different levels of health care for daughters versus sons.
  \end{block}
  }

  \uncover<+->{
  \begin{block}{Suggested Findings}
  \bi
  \item<+-> Bias occurs via health care costs.
  \item<+-> Bias occurs even in the absence of costs (inherent bias).
  \item<+-> Stronger evidence in favor of the bias through the cost channel.
  \ei
  \end{block}
  }
}

\frame
{
  \ft{Health Care Cost Channel}
  \uncover<+->{
  \begin{block}{Interpretation of the Cost channel}
  \bi
  \item<+-> Measuring the response in health care decision to an increase in price (slope or elasticity of a demand curve).
  \item<+-> Author is misinterpreting on interaction term Price*Male.
  \item<+-> Add together interaction with Price coefficient, response to sons is actually \textit{positive}.
  \ei
  \end{block}
  }

  \uncover<+->{
  \begin{block}{Endogeneity Problem}
  \bi
  \item<+-> Not estimating demand (any more than a supply curve).
  \item<+-> Need to identify shifters for supply of health care to trace point on demand curve.
  \item<+-> Instrumental Variable Regression: Identify variables that influence supply to instrument for price.
  \ei
  \end{block}
  }
}

\frame
{
  \ft{Inherent Bias}
  \bi
  \item<+-> Besides a difference in response to price, is there a bias against daughters?
  \item<+-> Interpretation: relative \textit{position} of the demand curve for daughters 
  \item<+-> Absence of Price*Male interaction in regression equations does not imply an absence of cost.
  \item<+-> Cannot interpret the regressions in Tables 5, 5a, 6a as response in health care when costs are negligible. 
  \ei
}

\frame
{
  \ft{Other Comments}
  \uncover<+->{
  \begin{block}{Marginal Effects}
  \bi
  \item<+-> Question computation/interpretation for a marginal effect with interaction terms.
  \item<+-> Cross partial marginal effects are tough, see Ai and Norton, Economic Letters, 1993.
  \item<+-> Are marginal effects of dummy variables computed properly?
  \ei
  \end{block}
  }

  \uncover<+->{
  \begin{block}{Complex Interactions}
   Male*Price*Hindu*hprop etc... too complex, of little value.
  \end{block}
  }
}
